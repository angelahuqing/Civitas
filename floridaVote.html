Florida
In Florida a person must be registered to vote at least 29 days before the actual election date. A person can either register to vote in person or by mail by completing the Florida Voter Registration Application and giving or sending it to your local county Supervisor of Elections. To vote in the primary election which determines the candidate that will represent each party, a person must be a registered member of that party. The next general election is November 6, 2018; it will fill the Senate seat currently held by Bill Nelson. Go to this page to register to vote. A person only needs to register once unless they change their name or change addresses.
