Bill Nelson

Florida State Senator



Key Issues:

  • Florida Hurricanes: When Hurricanes Charley, Frances, Ivan, and Jennie happened Senator Nelson made sure to help secure $14.5 billion in federal aid for the victims.

  • Child Care: Senator Nelson believes in the continued protection of children and their health. In 2003 Senator Nelson asked the EPA to look into and determine if arsenic-treated wood, which is found in playgrounds across the country, is a major child risk. Senator Nelson then created legislation to stop the use of arsenic wood in playgrounds.

  • Veterans: In the 1960s there was a study that was performed on U.S, So Bill made the Defense Department to give information about the chemical and biological weapon testing. Bill took a stand and right away he called for immediate treatment for the veterans that went through the testing and been harmed.

Background:

Nelson was born September 29, 1942 in Miami Florida. He was educated at University of Florida, Yale University for his BA, and University of Virginia for his JD. Before he was elected to the senate, he was a Treasurer, a Insurance Commissioner, and a Fire Marshal of Florida from Jan 3, 1995- Jan 3, 2001. He was also elected to the U.S House of the Representatives for several terms. He also served in the US army for several years. Nelson has been in his current office since January 3, 2001.
